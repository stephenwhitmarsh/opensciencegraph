# Euro-BioImaging ERIC

## Overview
Euro-BioImaging is the European Research Infrastructure Consortium for biological
and biomedical imaging, established as an ERIC in 2019 after entering the ESFRI
Roadmap in 2008 and receiving ESFRI Landmark status in 2018. It provides open
access to state-of-the-art imaging technologies, training, and data services
across 41 Nodes comprising 295 facilities in 18 countries and the EMBL. Its
scope spans the full imaging spectrum — from super-resolution and light-sheet
fluorescence microscopy at the cellular scale to clinical MRI and PET at the
organism scale — making it the single largest open-access imaging infrastructure
in Europe and the direct European counterpart of [[04_Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]].

## Structure
Euro-BioImaging operates through a distributed Hub model:
- **Seat** — Turku, Finland (Director General: John Eriksson)
- **Bio-Hub** — EMBL Heidelberg, Germany (biological imaging)
- **Med-Hub** — Turin, Italy (medical imaging, including MRI, PET, SPECT)
- **41 Nodes** — distributed across Europe, each specialising in specific
  imaging technologies; researchers access nodes directly via the Hub portal

## Open Science and Data Services
- Mandate to promote open access to imaging instrumentation and expertise
- Image data services including FAIR data management, deposition support,
  and cloud-based image analysis
- Strong collaboration with [[01_Actors/OME\|OME]] for data format standardisation
  (OME-TIFF, OME-Zarr) and OMERO deployment at nodes
- Partners with the BioImage Archive (EMBL-EBI) for image data deposition
- Active participant in [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]] and the Global BioImaging network
- Working relationship with [[04_Governance/QUAREP-LiMi\|QUAREP-LiMi]] for microscopy QC standards
