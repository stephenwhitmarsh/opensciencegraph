# ECRIN — European Clinical Research Infrastructure Network

## Overview
ECRIN (European Clinical Research Infrastructure Network) is a European research
infrastructure that supports the conduct of multinational, non-commercial clinical
trials and other clinical research studies across Europe. Established in 2004 and
becoming an ERIC in 2013, ECRIN provides regulatory expertise, data management
support, and a network of national partner organisations that facilitate the
practical aspects of running clinical studies across multiple European countries —
from regulatory submissions and ethics approvals to data collection and monitoring.

For [[00_Core/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]], ECRIN is directly relevant through [[04_Governance/AP-HP\|AP-HP]]
as the French national partner, and through ICM's participation in multinational
neurological disease trials that require pan-European site coordination.

## What ECRIN Does

### Multinational Trial Support
ECRIN's core service is enabling non-commercial (academic) clinical trials to
recruit patients across multiple European countries simultaneously:
- **Regulatory support** — navigating different national competent authorities
  (ANSM in France, MHRA in UK, BfArM in Germany, etc.) for multinational CTA submissions
- **Ethics coordination** — supporting parallel ethics (CPP) submissions across countries
- **National partner network** — ECRIN's national partners (NCPs) are embedded in
  national clinical research networks and handle local logistics

### Data Management and Standards
ECRIN promotes harmonised clinical trial data standards:
- **[[02_Standards/CDISC\|CDISC]]** — ECRIN endorses CDISC SDTM/ADaM/CDASH for trial data structure
- **[[02_Standards/OMOP CDM\|OMOP CDM]]** — ECRIN uses OMOP for harmonising observational data across studies
- **[[02_Standards/HL7 FHIR\|HL7 FHIR]]** — ECRIN contributes to FHIR-based clinical trial interoperability
- **[[03_Platforms/ClinicalTrials.gov\|ClinicalTrials.gov]]** and **EudraCT/CTIS** — registration support

### CTIS and EudraCT
Under the EU Clinical Trials Regulation (EU 536/2014), ECRIN is a key actor in
the transition to **CTIS** (Clinical Trials Information System):
- EudraCT is being phased out (fully replaced by CTIS from January 2025)
- ECRIN provides training and support for sponsors navigating the CTIS submission process
- CTIS is a single EU-wide portal for clinical trial applications, replacing national
  submissions to each member state separately

### REDCap Network
ECRIN operates the **ECRIN REDCap Network** — a secure, GDPR-compliant instance
of the REDCap electronic data capture system available to ECRIN-supported studies.
REDCap provides eCRF (electronic Case Report Form) functionality for multisite studies.

## French Partnership
The French national partner is **F-CRIN** (French Clinical Research Infrastructure
Network), coordinated by **Inserm** and the French CIC (Centre d'Investigation
Clinique) network embedded in [[04_Governance/AP-HP\|AP-HP]] and other major French university hospitals.
F-CRIN connects ECRIN to:
- [[04_Governance/AP-HP\|AP-HP]] CICs (including Pitié-Salpêtrière CIC Neurosciences)
- Inserm CIC network (30+ sites across France)
- GIRCI (Groupements Interrégionaux de Recherche Clinique et d'Innovation)

## ESFRI Cluster
ECRIN is part of the ESFRI Health Research Infrastructure cluster with [[04_Governance/ELIXIR\|ELIXIR]],
[[04_Governance/BBMRI-ERIC\|BBMRI-ERIC]], [[04_Governance/EATRIS\|EATRIS]], and [[04_Governance/Euro-BioImaging\|Euro-BioImaging]].
