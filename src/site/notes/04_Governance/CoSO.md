# CoSO — Comité pour la Science Ouverte

## Overview
The Comité pour la Science Ouverte (CoSO) is the French interministerial committee
responsible for coordinating and implementing the national open science policy under
[[04_Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]]. Created in 2018 by the Ministry of Higher Education and
Research (MESR), CoSO brings together representatives from all major French research
organisations — CNRS, Inserm, ANR, CPU/France Universités, IRD, INRAE, and others
— to develop guidelines, promote best practices, and track progress on open science
implementation across French publicly funded research.

CoSO is essentially the operational governance body of [[04_Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]]:
it drafts the policy documents, coordinates thematic working groups, manages the
(Fonds National pour la Science Ouverte), and interfaces with European
open science initiatives ([[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]], [[04_Governance/OpenAIRE\|OpenAIRE]]).

## Structure: Four Colleges
CoSO operates through four thematic colleges, each addressing a pillar of
[[04_Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]]:

### College Publications
- Open access to scientific publications
- Implementation of Plan S / cOAlition S in France
- Diamond open access journal support via the FNSO (Fonds National pour la Science Ouverte)
- HAL mandate compliance and [[04_Governance/HAL\|HAL]] institutional portals
- Preprint policies and rights retention strategies

### College Research Data
- [[04_Governance/FAIR Principles\|FAIR Principles]] implementation for French research data
- [[03_Platforms/Recherche Data Gouv\|Recherche Data Gouv]] development and governance
- DMP (Data Management Plan) mandates via [[04_Governance/OPIDoR\|OPIDoR]]
- Disciplinary repository alignment ([[03_Platforms/OpenNeuro\|OpenNeuro]], [[03_Platforms/EGA\|EGA]], [[03_Platforms/NCBI GEO\|NCBI GEO]])
- [[04_Governance/EHDS\|EHDS]] national implementation for health data

### College Open Software
- Open source software publication mandates
- Software Heritage deposit recommendations
- Code citation practices
- ORCID and software persistent identifiers

### College Competences and Training
- Open science literacy in researcher training (doctoral schools)
- Data steward profession development
- [[04_Governance/RDMkit\|RDMkit]] and [[04_Governance/OPIDoR\|OPIDoR]] DORANum training resources
- Evaluation reform (moving beyond impact factors — San Francisco Declaration / DORA)

## Key Outputs
- **Second National Plan for Open Science (2021–2024)** — CoSO drafted and monitors
  implementation; see [[04_Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]]
- **FNSO calls for projects** — CoSO manages the National Fund for Open Science
  (Fonds National pour la Science Ouverte / FNSO), funding diamond OA journals, open
  software, and open science training initiatives
- **Open science barometer guidance** — CoSO coordinates with [[Barometre Science
  Ouverte]] for monitoring
- **EOSC engagement** — CoSO coordinates French participation in [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]] governance
  and service development

## Membership
CoSO includes representatives from:
- MESR (Ministry) — secretariat
- CNRS, Inserm, IRD, INRAE, INRIA, CEA, IFREMER — major research organisations
- ANR — national research agency (funder)
- France Universités (CPU) — university network
- CDEFI — engineering school network
- RENATER — national research network
- [[04_Governance/IFB\|IFB]], CCSD (HAL operator — Centre pour la Communication Scientifique Directe) — infrastructure operators

[[00_Core/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] participates in CoSO activities through its parent
organisations CNRS and Inserm.
