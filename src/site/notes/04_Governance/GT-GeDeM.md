# GT-GeDeM — Groupe de Travail Gestion des Données en Microscopie

## Overview
GT-GeDeM (Gestion des Données en Microscopie) is a working group of the
**RTmfm** (Réseau Technologique Microscopie Fluorescence Multidimensionnelle),
the French CNRS technological network for optical microscopy. RTmfm itself
is funded by CNRS MITI and brings together microscopy platform personnel,
researchers, and engineers across France involved in the development and
use of optical microscopy. GT-GeDeM's specific mandate is to facilitate
the transition towards FAIR-compliant microscopy data management in French
imaging platforms. **[[01_Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] participates in this
working group.**

## Mandate and Objectives
GT-GeDeM works from the principle that correct and complete annotation of
data and metadata is fundamental for scientific traceability, underpins
peer review, guarantees reproducibility, and positions data as a reusable
resource. Its four objectives are:

1. **Survey** — inventory the needs and solutions already in place at
   different imaging platforms across France
2. **Guidelines** — develop guidelines for harmonised, complete FAIR
   annotation of microscopy data and for assembling Data Management Plans
3. **Infrastructure advice** — provide guidance on informatics tools and
   infrastructure solutions for microscopy data management
4. **Training** — organise training around data management solutions
   dedicated to imaging platform staff

## Context within RTmfm
GT-GeDeM is one of several working groups within RTmfm, alongside:
- **WG-Quality Control / GT-3M** — metrology and QC (connected to [[04_Governance/QUAREP-LiMi\|QUAREP-LiMi]])
- **GT-MAIIA** — AI methods for image analysis
- **GT-Expansion** — expansion microscopy
- **GT-Transparisation** — tissue clearing

RTmfm partners with [[04_Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] and GDR Imabio for the annual
"Assises des plateformes d'Imagerie" gathering of French microscopy platform staff.
