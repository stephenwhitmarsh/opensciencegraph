# ARDC — Australian Research Data Commons

## Overview
The Australian Research Data Commons (ARDC) is Australia's national digital research infrastructure facility, established in 2018 by merging three predecessor organisations: the Australian National Data Service (ANDS), Nectar (National eResearch Collaboration Tools and Resources), and Research Data Services (RDS). Funded through the Australian Government's **National Collaborative Research Infrastructure Strategy (NCRIS)**, ARDC provides Australian researchers with competitive advantage through data — enabling access to nationally significant eInfrastructure, platforms, skills, and high-quality data collections.

ARDC is the Australian counterpart to [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]] (European Open Science Cloud) and [[03_Platforms/Recherche Data Gouv\|Recherche Data Gouv]] — and has been explicitly named alongside EOSC and NIH Data Commons in international research data commons convergence discussions at the **Research Data Alliance (RDA)**.

## Key Services and Programmes

### Research Data Australia
ARDC manages **Research Data Australia** (RDA), a web portal enabling access to data from 100+ Australian research organisations, cultural institutions, and government agencies. Metadata is described using standardised schemas aligned with [[02_Standards/DCAT\|DCAT]] and [[02_Standards/Dublin Core\|Dublin Core]], enabling discoverability and EOSC interoperability.

### Nectar Research Cloud
A national, freely available cloud computing infrastructure for Australian researchers, with 50,000+ core capacity. It provides virtual laboratories and analytical platforms for data-intensive research.

### FAIR Data Infrastructure
ARDC is Australia's primary FAIR implementation body: **FAIR Data 101** — open training course on FAIR principles; **FAIR Data Self-Assessment Tool** — interactive FAIRness evaluation; **Australian National PID Strategy (2024)** — coordinates persistent identifier adoption (DOI, ORCID, ROR) across Australian research sector; **Controlled vocabularies** — ARDC RVA service for domain-specific vocabulary publishing and management.

### Domain Data Commons
ARDC supports discipline-specific data commons including: **Biological Psychiatry Data Commons** (2025) — FAIR-aligned psychiatric omics platform; uses Gen3 platform; led by Bioplatforms Australia and Australian BioCommons; includes ARDC, Phenomics Australia; directly relevant to the neuroimaging + omics intersection explored at [[01_Actors/The Florey\|The Florey]]; **Planet Research Data Commons** — environmental / ecological data; **People Research Data Commons** — health, social, and population research data.

### International Alignment
ARDC actively engages with international open science initiatives: **[[04_Governance/RDA\|RDA]]** — ARDC is an RDA member and participant in RDA plenaries; explicitly named alongside EOSC, NIH Data Commons, and the African Open Science Platform in RDA international convergence discussions; **[[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]]** — bilateral alignment confirmed; EC has identified EOSC-ARDC coordination as a priority; **ICRI 2024** — ARDC hosted the International Conference on Research Infrastructures in Brisbane (2024) in partnership with the European Commission.

## Relevance to Neuroscience / ICM Context
ARDC provides the national data infrastructure within which [[01_Actors/The Florey\|The Florey]]'s Australian Epilepsy Project (AEP) operates. The AEP data platform was presented at ARDC's eResearch Australasia conference as an example of FAIR-aligned, community-driven biomedical data infrastructure. ARDC's Biological Psychiatry Data Commons directly parallels the psychiatric genomics work of [[01_Actors/Mount Sinai Neuroscience\|Mount Sinai Neuroscience]]'s PsychENCODE and the mental health data sharing frameworks developing through [[04_Governance/HDR UK\|HDR UK]] in the UK and [[04_Governance/Health Data Hub\|Health Data Hub]] in France.
