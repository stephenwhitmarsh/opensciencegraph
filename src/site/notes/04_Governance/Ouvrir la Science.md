# Ouvrir la Science - French National Open Science Plan

## Overview
Ouvrir la Science is the French national open science plan (2018, updated 2021-2024).
It mandates open access to publications, FAIR research data deposit, open source
software publication, and transformed research evaluation. Implemented by [[04_Governance/CoSO\|CoSO]]
and enforced through [[04_Governance/ANR Open Science Policy\|ANR Open Science Policy]].

## Key Pillars (2021–2024) — Second PNSO
The Second National Plan for Open Science (2021–2024, https://www.ouvrirlascience.fr/second-national-plan-for-open-science-npos/)
expanded the original 2018 PNSO with four pillars:
1. **Open access to publications** — via [[04_Governance/HAL\|HAL]]; cOAlition S / Plan S alignment
2. **FAIR research data** — via [[03_Platforms/Recherche Data Gouv\|Recherche Data Gouv]]; mandatory DMPs via [[04_Governance/OPIDoR\|OPIDoR]]
3. **Open source software** — Software Heritage deposits; code citation
4. **Transform research practices and incentives** — open science in hiring/evaluation

### Fonds National pour la Science Ouverte (FNSO)
The **National Fund for Open Science** (https://www.ouvrirlascience.fr/national-fund-for-open-science/)
was created alongside the Second PNSO to provide dedicated funding for open
science infrastructure and practices, including support for diamond open access
journals, open software, and open science training.
