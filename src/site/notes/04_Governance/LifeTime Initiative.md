# LifeTime Initiative

## Overview
LifeTime is a European FET Flagship candidate initiative that aims to use single-cell technologies, multi-omics, and imaging to intercept and treat human diseases at their earliest stages, before symptoms arise. Published as a flagship science paper in Nature (Rajewsky et al. 2020), LifeTime brings together 90+ leading European research institutions to build the scientific and technological foundations for a new era of personalised, predictive medicine based on single-cell molecular profiling across human tissues.

LifeTime is on the **ESFRI roadmap** (2021 update) as a candidate landmark infrastructure, signalling European commitment to building the necessary infrastructure for cell-base disease interception at scale.

## Scientific Vision
LifeTime's core premise is that disease can be intercepted at the single-cell level before clinical manifestation — if we can characterise cell states across healthy and diseased human tissues at sufficient resolution and scale. The initiative focuses on:

- **Single-cell multi-omics** — scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq, spatial transcriptomics, proteomics applied to human tissue atlases in health and disease
- **Disease interception** — identifying cell state transitions that precede clinical disease in neurological, cardiovascular, metabolic, and oncological conditions
- **Personalised medicine** — using single-cell profiles to stratify patients and predict treatment response
- **Organoids and in vitro models** — patient-derived organoid systems for drug testing and mechanistic studies

## Relevance to Neuroscience
Neurological and neuropsychiatric diseases are a central focus area for LifeTime:
- **Alzheimer's and Parkinson's** — single-cell characterisation of disease progression in human brain tissue; alignment with [[01_Actors/BICAN\|BICAN]] cell atlas reference data
- **Multiple sclerosis** — single-cell immune and glial profiling
- **Psychiatric disorders** — single-cell genomics of brain circuits

The LifeTime data infrastructure aligns with [[03_Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]] (brain data), [[04_Governance/BBMRI-ERIC\|BBMRI-ERIC]] (tissue biobanks), and [[04_Governance/ELIXIR\|ELIXIR]] (bioinformatics), and uses AnnData/h5ad format for single-cell data alongside [[02_Standards/VCF\|VCF]] for associated variant data.
