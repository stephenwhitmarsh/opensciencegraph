# YODA Project — Yale Open Data Access

## Overview
The Yale University Open Data Access (YODA) Project is an independent, academic initiative housed at Yale University that facilitates responsible sharing of individual participant-level clinical trial data. Founded in 2013 by Joseph Ross and Harlan Krumholz at Yale School of Medicine, YODA operates as a trusted third-party intermediary between industry data holders and external researchers — taking full jurisdiction over data access decisions, removing conflicts of interest from the data sharing process.

The YODA Project's mission is to promote open science and research transparency by making clinical trial data available for secondary analysis, meta-analysis, and replication while protecting participant confidentiality and ensuring good data stewardship.

## Model and Scope
YODA's data-sharing model is distinctive:
- **Independent jurisdiction** — data partners contractually transfer full decision-making authority to YODA; the Project, not the data owner, grants or denies access
- **499 trials available** (as of 2024) — predominantly pharmaceutical and medical device trials from partners including Johnson & Johnson and SI-BONE
- **385 requests processed** — ~95% approval rate; all requests and decisions publicly posted for transparency
- **Secure enclave access** — approved researchers access data alongside protocols, analysis plans, and clinical study reports (CSRs) in a secure environment

## Data Access Process
1. Researcher submits proposal with analysis plan via YODA website
2. Review by YODA Steering Committee + blinded review by data partner
3. Conflict of interest disclosure required
4. Data Use Agreement (DUA) signed prior to access
5. All requests, decisions, and outcomes publicly posted

## Policy Influence
YODA has had direct influence on US and international data sharing policy:
- Informed the **NIH Data Sharing Policy** strengthening (enacted 2023), which now requires all NIH-funded investigators to share data
- Provided proof-of-concept that large-scale clinical trial data sharing is feasible without "the sky falling" — changing minds in the pharmaceutical industry
- Co-founders Ross and Krumholz subsequently co-founded **medRxiv** (see [[03_Platforms/Preprint Servers\|Preprint Servers]]), extending their open science mission to preprints

## Relevance to ICM
YODA is relevant to [[01_Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] clinical research in two ways:
- **Data access**: ICM clinical investigators may apply for access to YODA-held trial data for secondary analyses in neurological or psychiatric conditions
- **Model**: YODA's governance model (independent third-party DAC, public posting of decisions, standardised DUAs) is a reference for clinical data sharing policy development in France and Europe, including [[04_Governance/ECRIN\|ECRIN]] and
  [[04_Governance/Health Data Hub\|Health Data Hub]] frameworks
