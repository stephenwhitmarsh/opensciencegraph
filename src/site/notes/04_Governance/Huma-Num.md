# Huma-Num — Infrastructure Nationale de Recherche en Sciences Humaines et Sociales

## Overview
Huma-Num is the French national research infrastructure for digital humanities
and social sciences (SHS — Sciences Humaines et Sociales), operated by CNRS
in partnership with Aix-Marseille Université and Campus Condorcet. Formally
established as a TGIR (Très Grande Infrastructure de Recherche) in 2013 and
elevated to IR* (Infrastructure de Recherche étoilée) status, it provides
digital tools, services, and expertise for managing, preserving, and sharing
research data in the humanities and social sciences.

While primarily serving humanities disciplines, Huma-Num is relevant to
[[00_Core/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] through its overlap with cognitive and social
neuroscience, its connection to the broader French open science infrastructure,
and its role as a French node of the European **DARIAH** (Digital Research
Infrastructure for the Arts and Humanities) and **CLARIN** (Common Language
Resources and Technology Infrastructure) ERICs.

## Key Services
- **Nakala** — persistent storage and publication service for research data and
  collections; assigns persistent identifiers (handles); exports to
  [[03_Platforms/Recherche Data Gouv\|Recherche Data Gouv]] catalogue
- **Isidore** — search platform aggregating French and European SHS open access
  publications and data
- **Sharedocs** — collaborative document management
- **Stylo** — semantic text editor for scholarly writing with structured metadata
- **HumaNum Box** — collaborative file sharing and storage for research teams
- **Consortium support** — Huma-Num supports 30+ national discipline-specific
  consortia (CAHIER, CORLI, etc.) for data standardisation in their fields

## DARIAH and CLARIN Membership
Huma-Num is the French node of two European ERICs:
- **DARIAH-EU** — digital methods and infrastructure for arts and humanities
  research across Europe
- **CLARIN-EU** — language resources and technology infrastructure for
  linguistics and language-based research

## Relevance to Neuroscience
The overlap with neuroscience is primarily through:
- **Cognitive neuroscience** — studies of language, memory, and behaviour that
  bridge neuroscience and humanities methodology
- **Open science alignment** — Huma-Num implements [[04_Governance/FAIR Principles\|FAIR Principles]] and
  [[04_Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]] for SHS; model for cross-domain open science practice
- **Digital humanities tools** — applicable to qualitative research and patient
  narrative data in clinical neuroscience contexts
