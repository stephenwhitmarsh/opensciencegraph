# European Commission Open Science Policy

## Overview
The European Commission's open science policy is the overarching EU framework
mandating open access, FAIR data, and open science practices for all EU-funded
research. Implemented through DG Research and Innovation (DG RTD), it provides
the policy foundation for the [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]] ecosystem, shapes Horizon Europe grant
conditions, and drives the [[04_Governance/EHDS\|EHDS]] regulatory framework.

The EC open science policy operates through several instruments: mandatory grant
conditions in Horizon Europe, the EOSC Partnership, EU legislation (EHDS, Data
Governance Act, Data Act), and soft law guidance (recommendations, communications).

## Key Policy Instruments

### Horizon Europe Open Science Requirements (2021–2027)
All Horizon Europe projects are required to:
- Make publications open access immediately upon publication (Plan S / cOAlition S)
- Produce a Data Management Plan (DMP) within 6 months of project start
- Make research data "as open as possible, as closed as necessary" under [[04_Governance/FAIR Principles\|FAIR Principles]]
- Deposit data in [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]]-compatible repositories (e.g. [[03_Platforms/Zenodo\|Zenodo]], [[03_Platforms/OpenNeuro\|OpenNeuro]],
  [[03_Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]], [[03_Platforms/EGA\|EGA]] for sensitive data)
- Follow the European Code of Conduct for Research Integrity (ALLEA)

### European Open Science Cloud (EOSC)
The [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]] is the operational implementation of EC open science policy —
a federated infrastructure for open science data across Europe. The EOSC
Association governs the EOSC ecosystem, with [[04_Governance/OpenAIRE\|OpenAIRE]], [[04_Governance/ELIXIR\|ELIXIR]],
[[03_Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]], [[04_Governance/Euro-BioImaging\|Euro-BioImaging]], and others as service providers.

### Plan S / cOAlition S
The EC co-leads **cOAlition S** (with national funders including [[04_Governance/ANR Open Science Policy\|ANR Open Science Policy]]),
implementing **Plan S** — the requirement that all publicly funded research
be published open access without embargo from 2021. Rights Retention Strategy
(RRS) allows authors to retain rights for immediate open-access deposit even
in subscription journals.

### EOSC EU Node
The **EOSC EU Node** (https://open-science-cloud.ec.europa.eu) is the European
Commission's own contribution to the EOSC ecosystem — providing direct cloud
storage and computing resources for EU-funded research data, operated by the
Commission alongside national and institutional contributions.

## Alignment with French Policy
French open science policy ([[04_Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]], [[04_Governance/ANR Open Science Policy\|ANR Open Science Policy]])
is explicitly aligned with EC open science requirements:
- ANR mandates mirror Horizon Europe DMP and OA requirements
- [[04_Governance/CoSO\|CoSO]] coordinates French implementation of EC policy commitments
- [[03_Platforms/Recherche Data Gouv\|Recherche Data Gouv]] is France's contribution to the EOSC data space
