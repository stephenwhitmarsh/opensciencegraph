# NBO — Neurobehavior Ontology

## Overview
The Neurobehavior Ontology is an OBO Foundry ontology providing a controlled
vocabulary for the description of behavioural and neurological phenotypes in
both humans and model organisms. NBO covers the full spectrum of neurobehavioural
assessments: motor function, sensory processing, cognitive performance, social
behaviour, anxiety, aggression, and reward. It is designed to be used in
conjunction with [[02_Standards/HPO\|HPO]] (human phenotypic abnormalities) and the Mammalian
Phenotype Ontology (MP) for model organism phenotypes, and integrates with
[[02_Standards/UBERON\|UBERON]] for anatomical localisation of behavioural functions.

For [[01_Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]], NBO is relevant to the behavioural phenotyping
work conducted at the **PRISME** platform and to the annotation of cognitive
and behavioural assessments in clinical cohorts such as MEMENTO and INSIGHT-preAD.

## Key Concept Areas
- **Motor behaviours** — locomotion, gait, balance, tremor, coordination
- **Cognitive behaviours** — learning, memory, attention, executive function
- **Sensory behaviours** — pain sensitivity, olfaction, vision, audition
- **Social and affective behaviours** — anxiety, depression-like behaviour,
  social interaction, aggression, reward and addiction
- **Sleep and circadian behaviours** — sleep architecture, circadian rhythm

## Usage in Neuroscience
- **Preclinical phenotyping** — annotation of mouse model behavioural data
  at platforms like PRISME; NBO terms link behavioural readouts to disease models
- **Clinical cohort annotation** — standardising description of behavioural
  and cognitive assessments across multisite studies
- **Cross-species comparison** — NBO bridges human clinical behavioural terms
  with model organism phenotype descriptions (MP, Rat Genome Database)
- **FAIR data annotation** — structured behavioural metadata enables
  cross-study comparison and federated analyses
