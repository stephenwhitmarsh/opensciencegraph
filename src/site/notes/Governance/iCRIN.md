# iCRIN — Infrastructure for Clinical Research in Neurosciences

## Overview
iCRIN (Infrastructure for Clinical Research in Neurosciences) is [[Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]]'s translational clinical research programme, designed to bridge ICM's basic research teams with the clinical neurology and psychiatry services of the AP-HP DMU (Département Médico-Universitaire) Neurosciences at Pitié-Salpêtrière Hospital. Launched in 2019 with 13 thematic projects selected by ICM's Scientific Advisory Board, iCRIN serves as an accelerator for clinical research at the interface of basic neuroscience and patient care — building cohorts, developing biomarkers, and designing proof-of-concept studies across all major neurological and psychiatric disease areas treated at Pitié-Salpêtrière.

iCRIN is distinct from the [[Governance/CIC Neurosciences\|CIC Neurosciences]] (CIC-1422), which provides the formal Inserm/AP-HP regulatory and operational infrastructure for clinical trials. iCRIN uses the CIC as its operational backbone while organising research activity thematically across disease areas and connecting clinical teams with ICM researchers.

## Structure and Thematic Projects
iCRIN is organised around 13 disease-area themes, each a collaboration between an AP-HP clinical service and one or more ICM research teams:

- **Alzheimer's disease** — in collaboration with IM2A (Institut de la Mémoire et de la Maladie d'Alzheimer); cohort building, cognitive markers, biomarker-enriched early-phase trials
- **Parkinson's disease** — familial forms cohort, prodromal biomarkers (CSF, imaging, biofluids), genetic risk
- **Epilepsy** — electro-clinico-biological characterisation; autoimmune epilepsy; digital tools (rEPI, EpiDay)
- **Stroke** — acute stroke biomarkers, neuroimaging endpoints, neuromodulation rehabilitation
- **ALS / MND** — biomarker development; therapeutic trial design
- **Multiple sclerosis** — biomarkers, imaging, neuromodulation
- **Neurogenetics** — rare neurological diseases; genotype-phenotype correlation
- **Neuro-oncology** — brain tumour biomarkers and therapeutic strategies
- **Psychiatry** — digital phenotyping, neuromodulation (rTMS/tDCS), mood disorders, PTSD, ASD
- **Neuromodulation / rehabilitation** — therapeutic video games, Living Lab collaboration, digital rehabilitation tools
- Additional themes covering movement disorders, pain, and neuroinflammation

## Role in the Clinical Research Ecosystem
iCRIN integrates several ICM infrastructures:
- **[[Governance/CIC Neurosciences\|CIC Neurosciences]]** (CIC-1422) — operational backbone for trial conduct, regulatory submissions, patient recruitment
- **[[Actors/CENIR\|CENIR]]** — neuroimaging endpoints (MRI, PET-MRI, MEG) for iCRIN studies
- **[[Actors/Banque ADN et Cellules\|Banque ADN et Cellules]]** — biobanking of cohort samples (DNA, CSF, plasma, cells)
- **[[Actors/DAC\|DAC]]** — bioinformatics and statistical analysis of iCRIN cohort data
- **[[Platforms/REDCap\|REDCap]]** — clinical data capture for iCRIN observational studies and trial databases
