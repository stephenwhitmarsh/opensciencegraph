# TransCelerate BioPharma

## Overview
TransCelerate BioPharma is a non-profit industry consortium of major pharmaceutical companies (20+ members including AstraZeneca, Bristol Myers Squibb, Eli Lilly, GSK, Johnson & Johnson, Novartis, Pfizer, Roche, Sanofi, and others) established in 2012 with the mission to improve the efficiency, quality, and cost-effectiveness of biopharmaceutical clinical research. Rather than competing on trial operations, Thranscelerate members collaborate on shared challenges that affect the entire industry.

## Key Programmes

### Data Standards and Interoperability
- **Common Protocol Template (CPT)** — standardised clinical trial protocol structure based on [[Standards/CDISC\|CDISC]] CDASH/SDTM; reduces protocol development time and variability
- **Data Exchange Standards** — promotes [[Standards/CDISC\|CDISC]] SDTM/ADaM for regulatory submissions; facilitates data sharing between sponsors and CROs
- **Digital Data Flow** — initiative to enable end-to-end digital, standards-based data flow from protocol through to regulatory submission

### myTrials (Site Portal)
**myTrials** is TransCelerate's shared investigator site portal providing a single interface for site staff to access documentation, training, and regulatory information across multiple sponsor companies — reducing the administrative burden on clinical trial sites.

### Site Qualification and Feasibility
Shared site qualification database allowing sites to complete one profile used by multiple pharmaceutical sponsors, reducing duplicate feasibility questionnaires.

### Digital Pathology
TransCelerate has an active programme on digital pathology standards for clinical trial tissue biomarker assessments, working on image transfer and analysis standards aligned with [[Standards/DICOM\|DICOM]] and [[Standards/OME File Formats\|OME File Formats]].

### Decentralised Clinical Trials (DCT)
Post-pandemic acceleration of DCT frameworks — guidance on remote patient monitoring, telemedicine visits, and home nursing in clinical trials.

## Relevance to Neuroscience
TransCelerate's standardisation work directly affects neurological disease trials:
- [[Standards/CDISC\|CDISC]] Therapeutic Area Standards for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease were developed in collaboration with TransCelerate
- Site qualification sharing reduces burden on neurology sites like [[Governance/AP-HP\|AP-HP]] Pitié-Salpêtrière participating in industry-sponsored trials
