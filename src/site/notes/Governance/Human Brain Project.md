# Human Brain Project (HBP)

## Overview
The Human Brain Project was a 10-year EU FET (Future and Emerging Technologies) Flagship initiative (2013–2023), one of the two largest scientific projects ever funded by the European Union with a total budget of approximately €600 million. Its mission was to build a collaborative European research infrastructure for neuroscience, medicine, and computing, using neuroinformatics, simulation, and high-performance computing to advance understanding of the human brain.

[[Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] was a participating institution throughout the HBP, contributing neuroimaging, clinical, and computational neuroscience expertise.

The HBP's most significant legacy is [[Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]] — the open neuroscience research infrastructure it created and which continues to operate beyond the project's end.

## Key Achievements and Legacy

### EBRAINS Research Infrastructure
[[Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]] (https://ebrains.eu) is the primary legacy of the HBP — a permanent open research infrastructure providing:
- Brain atlas and data sharing services
- Simulation and HPC for computational neuroscience
- openMINDS metadata framework (see below)
- Live Papers (interactive reproducible publications)

### openMINDS
**openMINDS** ([[Standards/openMINDS\|openMINDS]]) is the HBP/EBRAINS metadata framework for standardising neuroscience research data — the required metadata schema for depositing data on [[Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]]. openMINDS covers experimental metadata (subjects, protocols, samples), file formats ([[Standards/BIDS\|BIDS]], [[Standards/NWB\|NWB]], [[Standards/OME File Formats\|OME File Formats]]), and data provenance.

### UBERON Contributions
The HBP contributed significantly to the development and application of [[Standards/UBERON\|UBERON]] (cross-species anatomy ontology) for brain region annotation in atlases and data models, establishing it as the standard for linking brain structure names across species in computational models and data repositories.

### Multi-Level Brain Atlas
The HBP created the **Julich Brain Atlas** — a cytoarchitectonic probabilistic atlas of the human brain at cellular resolution, integrated into [[Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]] and freely available. The atlas provides probability maps for 200+ brain areas and is linked to [[Standards/UBERON\|UBERON]] anatomy terms.

### Neuromorphic Computing
The HBP built two neuromorphic computing platforms — **SpiNNaker** (Manchester) and **BrainScaleS** (Heidelberg) — available via [[Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]] for energy-efficient neural simulation, relevant to computational neuroscience and AI hardware research.

### FAIR Neuroscience Data Sharing
The HBP established FAIR data sharing practices in neuroscience at a European scale — making [[Standards/BIDS\|BIDS]] and [[Standards/NWB\|NWB]] adoption a requirement for HBP data, driving the creation of [[Platforms/EBRAINS\|EBRAINS]] data sharing workflows, and contributing to [[Actors/INCF\|INCF]] standards governance.

## Timeline
- **2013** — HBP launched; 135 partner institutions; Chris Markram (EPFL) as director
- **2015** — Major restructuring following open letter from 800+ neuroscientists
  criticising original plan; focus shifted to infrastructure
- **2016–2020** — Infrastructure consolidation; EBRAINS pilot services; atlas development
- **2020** — EBRAINS officially launched as successor infrastructure
- **2023** — HBP project concluded; EBRAINS continues as independent ERIC candidate
