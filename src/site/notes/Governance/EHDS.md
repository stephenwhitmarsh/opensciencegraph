# EHDS - European Health Data Space Regulation

## Overview
Regulation (EU) 2025/327, adopted on 11 February 2025 and in force from 26 March 2025, establishes the European Health Data Space — the EU's first sector-specific data space and a cornerstone of the European Health Union. It creates a unified legal, technical, and governance framework for accessing, sharing, and reusing electronic health data (EHD) across all EU Member States, covering both primary use (direct patient care) and secondary use (research, innovation, policy, public health). For neuroscience research institutions such as [[Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]], the EHDS is the single most consequential EU regulatory development for health data access since GDPR.

## Two Pillars

### Primary Use
Empowers EU citizens with new rights over their personal health data: right to access, download, and transmit electronic health records across borders via the **MyHealth@EU** interoperability platform. Mandates EHR system interoperability and certification across all Member States.

### Secondary Use
Enables researchers, innovators, and policymakers to apply for access to anonymised health data held by public and private data holders through national **Health Data Access Bodies (HDABs)** and the **HealthData@EU** cross-border infrastructure. Data in scope includes EHRs, medical imaging, lab results, genomic data, clinical trial data, registries, and claims data.

## Implementation Timeline
- **March 2025** — Regulation enters into force; transition period begins
- **June 2025** — Member States appoint national Digital Health Authorities
- **March 2027** — Commission adopts key implementing acts
- **March 2029** — Secondary use rules apply for most data categories (EHRs, imaging, lab results); first cross-border primary use functionalities live
- **March 2031** — Secondary use extends to genomic and -omics data; imaging and lab results accessible cross-border
- **March 2034** — Third countries can apply to join HealthData@EU

## Key Bodies Established
- **EHDS Board** — two representatives per Member State; coordinates implementation
- **Health Data Access Bodies (HDABs)** — one per Member State; process secondary use requests and maintain national dataset catalogues
- **Digital Health Authorities** — national implementation oversight
- **MyHealth@EU** — primary use interoperability platform
- **HealthData@EU** — secondary use federated infrastructure

## Standards Mandated / Aligned
- [[Standards/HL7 FHIR\|HL7 FHIR]] — mandated as the interoperability standard for EHR data exchange
- [[Standards/OMOP CDM\|OMOP CDM]] — strongly aligned for secondary use data standardisation
- [[Standards/SNOMED CT\|SNOMED CT]] — core clinical terminology for EHR content
- Secondary use infrastructure designed to meet [[Governance/FAIR Principles\|FAIR Principles]] (F1, A1, I1, R1)
