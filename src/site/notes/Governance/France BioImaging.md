# France BioImaging (FBI)

## Overview
France BioImaging is the French national research infrastructure for biological and biomedical imaging at the cellular and subcellular scale, covering light microscopy (confocal, super-resolution, light-sheet, TIRF, FLIM), electron microscopy, and multimodal correlative imaging. Founded in 2012 under the Investissements d'Avenir programme (PIA), it federates 10+ advanced imaging nodes across French research institutions including Institut Pasteur, Institu Curie, IGBMC (Strasbourg), Bordeaux Imaging Center, and others. It is the
French national node of [[Governance/Euro-BioImaging\|Euro-BioImaging]] ERIC and the imaging node of [[Governance/ELIXIR\|ELIXIR]] France. It is the cellular/subcellular imaging counterpart to [[Governance/France Life Imaging\|France Life Imaging]] (which covers in vivo whole-body imaging).

## Key Activities
- Open access to advanced microscopy platforms for French and European
  researchers via the [[Governance/Euro-BioImaging\|Euro-BioImaging]] access programme
- OMERO deployment across nodes for FAIR image data management
  (using [[Actors/OME\|OME]] infrastructure)
- Image analysis support and training at node level
- Harmonisation of imaging protocols and metadata standards across nodes,
  aligned with [[Standards/OME File Formats\|OME File Formats]] and [[Governance/QUAREP-LiMi\|QUAREP-LiMi]] guidelines
- Coordination with [[Governance/France Life Imaging\|France Life Imaging]] for multimodal imaging pipelines

## Relevance to Paris Brain Institute
France BioImaging nodes cover brain-relevant imaging modalities including super-resolution microscopy of synaptic structures, expansion microscopy, light-sheet imaging of cleared brain tissue, and correlative light-electron microscopy — all directly relevant to [[Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] research programmes in cellular and circuit neuroscience.
