# NCBI GEO — Gene Expression Omnibus

## Overview
The Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) is the primary international public repository
for high-throughput functional genomics data, operated by NCBI/NIH since 2000.
It archives and freely distributes microarray, next-generation sequencing (NGS),
and other forms of high-throughput functional genomic data from submitted studies.
GEO is the de facto standard archive for gene expression data, and many journals
mandate GEO (or equivalent) submission as a condition of publication.

For [[01_Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]], GEO is relevant as a deposition target for
transcriptomics data generated by the [[01_Actors/iGENSEQ\|iGENSEQ]] platform, particularly
bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, and spatial transcriptomics datasets from
neurological disease studies.

## Data Types Accepted
- **Microarray** — gene expression, SNP genotyping, CGH arrays, ChIP-on-chip
- **RNA-seq** — bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq), spatial transcriptomics
- **ChIP-seq** — chromatin immunoprecipitation sequencing (histone marks, TF binding)
- **ATAC-seq** — chromatin accessibility
- **Bisulfite sequencing** — DNA methylation (WGBS, RRBS)
- **Other functional genomics** — CLIP-seq, Ribo-seq, Hi-C

## Structure
GEO organises data into three levels:
- **Platform (GPL)** — describes the array or sequencer used
- **Sample (GSM)** — a single biological sample; raw and processed data
- **Series (GSE)** — a collection of samples forming a study; the primary submission unit

Raw sequencing data (FASTQ files) associated with GEO submissions are typically
archived in **SRA (Sequence Read Archive)**, which is NCBI's companion repository
for raw reads, cross-linked from GEO series records.

## International Partners (INSDC)
GEO is part of the **International Nucleotide Sequence Database Collaboration
(INSDC)** along with:
- **[[03_Platforms/DDBJ\|DDBJ]]** — DNA Data Bank of Japan
- **ENA** — European Nucleotide Archive (EMBL-EBI)

These three databases mirror each other's sequence data, ensuring global
redundancy and access.

## Connections
- Operated by: NCBI / NIH
- Part of: INSDC (with [[03_Platforms/DDBJ\|DDBJ]] and ENA/EMBL-EBI)
- Raw data companion: SRA (Sequence Read Archive, NCBI)
- Complementary (controlled-access): [[03_Platforms/EGA\|EGA]], [[03_Platforms/dbGaP\|dbGaP]]
- Standards: [[02_Standards/VCF\|VCF]] (for variant data), [[02_Standards/GO\|GO]] (gene annotation)
- Relevant to: [[01_Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] (RNA-seq and transcriptomics deposition
  from [[01_Actors/iGENSEQ\|iGENSEQ]] platform; mandatory for journal publication)
