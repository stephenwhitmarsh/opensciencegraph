# Preprint Servers — bioRxiv and medRxiv

## Overview
**bioRxiv** and **medRxiv** are the two primary preprint servers for the life sciences and clinical/health research respectively. Together they form the dominant preprint infrastructure for neuroscience and biomedical research, enabling researchers to share manuscripts publicly before (or alongside) formal peer review. Both are operated as free, not-for-profit services.

### bioRxiv
- Founded: 2013 by Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory (CSHL)
- Scope: all biological sciences including neuroscience, genomics, cell biology, bioinformatics, ecology
- Key neuroscience use: preprints of neuroimaging studies, electrophysiology, systems neuroscience, computational neuroscience, genomics
- Many landmark open science papers in neuroscience appeared first on bioRxiv (BIDS, fMRIPrep, NWB, OpenNeuro papers)

### medRxiv
- Founded: 2019 by Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, Yale University, and BMJ
- Scope: clinical medicine, health sciences, epidemiology, public health
- Key neuroscience use: clinical neurology, psychiatric epidemiology, clinical trial results, neuroimaging clinical studies, population health
- Co-founded by Joseph Ross and Harlan Krumholz (also co-founders of [[Governance/YODA Project\|YODA Project]])
- Rapidly became the dominant preprint server for clinical research; cited in WHO evidence reviews and national clinical guidelines

## Open Science Role
Preprint servers are foundational open science infrastructure: **open access by default** — all preprints freely available immediately; **accelerated dissemination** — manuscripts shared months before journal publication; **DOI assignment** — persistent identifiers for citation and data linking; **version history** — all revisions preserved with distinct DOIs; **ORCID integration** — author identification aligned with FAIR principles; **recommended by journals** — Nature, PLOS, eLife, BMJ, and many others encourage or require preprint posting.

## Relevance to ICM / Paris Brain Institute
Both servers are directly relevant to ICM's open science workflows: **bioRxiv** is the primary preprint venue for ICM neuroimaging, genomics, and computational neuroscience outputs; it complements mandatory deposit in [[Platforms/HAL\|HAL]]. **medRxiv** is the primary preprint venue for ICM clinical neurology and clinical trial results; it complements [[Platforms/ClinicalTrials.gov\|ClinicalTrials.gov]] registration. French open science policy ([[Governance/Ouvrir la Science\|Ouvrir la Science]], [[Governance/ANR Open Science Policy\|ANR Open Science Policy]]) encourages preprint posting alongside [[Platforms/HAL\|HAL]] deposit; preprints on bioRxiv/medRxiv satisfy the immediate open access requirement when journal embargo periods apply.
