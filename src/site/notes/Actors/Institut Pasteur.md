# Institut Pasteur

## Overview
The Institut Pasteur is a private non-profit foundation dedicated to biomedical research, public health, and scientific education, located in Paris. One of the world's most prestigious research institutions, it has a strong and formally committed open science policy, adopting in May 2021 both a **Charter for Open Access to Publications** and a **Policy for the Management and Sharing of Research Data and Software Code** — making it one of the most advanced French research institutions on open science. As of 2024, 90% of Pasteurian publications are open access, compared to 67% nationally. It is directly relevant to [[Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] as a Paris-based peer institution within the same [[Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] and [[Governance/CNRS Open Science\|CNRS Open Science]] ecosystem.

## Open Science Commitment
- **2004** — signed the Berlin Declaration on Open Access
- **2021** — adopted formal Open Science Charter and Data/Software Policy
- **Pasteur 2030 strategic plan** — open science as one of eight core values
- **Barometer** — local adaptation of the French Open Science Monitor; tracks open access rates, data sharing, and software citation
- **Goal** — 100% open access publications from 2021 onwards
- All researchers required to deposit publications in HAL-Pasteur
- Research data managed according to [[Governance/FAIR Principles\|FAIR Principles]] (F, A, I, R pillars)

## Bioimaging
The Institut Pasteur is a founding node of [[Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] and hosts:
- **BioImage Analysis (BIA) unit** — develops image analysis and computer vision tools for multidimensional biological microscopy
- **Icy** — open-source bioimage analysis software developed at Pasteur, widely used across [[Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] nodes; plugins interoperate with [[Actors/OME\|OME]] tools
- Imaging platforms contributing to [[Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] Paris-Centre Node
- ABIDE II neuroimaging dataset: Institut Pasteur + Robert Debré Hospital contributed autism neuroimaging data, shared internationally
