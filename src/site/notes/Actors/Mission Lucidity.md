# Mission Lucidity

## Overview
Mission Lucidity is a Belgian neurodegenerative disease research consortium, founded in 2018 as a partnership between four Leuven-based research organisations:
- **imec** — world-leading nanoelectronics and digital technology research hub
- **KU Leuven** — leading European research university; strong neuroscience faculty
- **VIB** — Flanders Institute for Biotechnology; biomedical research institute
- **UZ Leuven** — University Hospital Leuven; clinical neurology

This unique combination of biomedical research (KU Leuven / VIB), clinical expertise (UZ Leuven), and advanced neurotechnology (imec) is Mission Lucidity's defining characteristic — applying nanoscale and digital technologies to decode neurodegenerative diseases. It operates a 10-year initiative with technology roadmaps and clear research horizons aimed at curing or preventing neurodegeneration.

Mission Lucidity is a **founding member** of the [[Governance/CURE-ND\|CURE-ND]] alliance alongside [[Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]], [[Actors/DZNE\|DZNE]], and [[Actors/UK DRI\|UK DRI]].

## Research Approach
Mission Lucidity takes a three-sided innovation platform:
- **Nanoscale technologies** — imec's nanoelectronics expertise applied to neural recording, brain-computer interfaces, and diagnostic biosensors
- **Human disease studies** — clinical research at UZ Leuven and KU Leuven on symptoms, biomarkers, and disease processes in patients
- **Brain activity modulation** — bridging cellular-level and systems-level understanding of neurodegeneration

Key research areas include Alzheimer's disease (spatial transcriptomics, single-cell omics), Parkinson's disease, and cerebral small vessel disease.

## Open Science Context
As a CURE-ND member, Mission Lucidity participates in shared ECR training, joint scientific workshops, and cross-institutional data and expertise exchange. VIB — one of Mission Lucidity's founding partners — has a strong open science tradition, including open-access publishing mandates and contribution to international consortia.
