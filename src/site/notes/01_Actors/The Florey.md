# The Florey — Florey Institute of Neuroscience and Mental Health

## Overview
The Florey Institute of Neuroscience and Mental Health is the largest brain research institute in the southern hemisphere, employing approximately 600 staff and students. Located across two sites in Melbourne's Parkville and Heidelberg, it is affiliated with the University of Melbourne, Austin Health, and the Royal Melbourne Hospital, and is embedded in the Melbourne Biomedical Precinct — one of the world's leading biomedical research hubs. The Florey investigates 20+ neurological and mental health conditions including Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, MND, epilepsy, multiple sclerosis, stroke, and depression.

Research is approximately 70% basic science and 30% translational, with access to MRI, MEG, and PET imaging facilities, histology, bioinformatics, and clinical trials infrastructure.

## Open Science Initiatives

### ENIGMA Consortium Participation
The Florey is a confirmed participant in the **[[01_Actors/ENIGMA Consortium\|ENIGMA Consortium]]** — specifically the **ENIGMA-Epilepsy** working group, which applies the ENIGMA federated meta-analysis model to structural and functional MRI data in epilepsy. Florey researchers appear as co-authors across multiple ENIGMA-Epilepsy publications, contributing multisite neuroimaging data through ENIGMA's coordinated analysis framework.

### Australian Epilepsy Project (AEP)
The Florey leads the **Australian Epilepsy Project** — a $30M national research platform funded by the Australian Government's Medical Research Future Fund (2021). The AEP:
- Integrates multimodal data: MRI, genetics, neuropsychology, AI-assisted analysis
- Builds a FAIR-aligned national data platform across five hub sites
- Was presented at the **ARDC eResearch Australasia** conference as a model for FAIR-aligned, community-driven biomedical data platforms
- Uses BIDS-compatible neuroimaging structures and interoperable data models
- Connects neuroimaging, genomics, and clinical data in a multisite Australian cohort

### ARDC Ecosystem
The Florey and AEP operate within Australia's national research data infrastructure, coordinated by the **[[04_Governance/ARDC\|ARDC]]** (Australian Research Data Commons). ARDC is Australia's FAIR data national infrastructure — the Australian counterpart to [[04_Governance/EOSC\|EOSC]] and [[03_Platforms/Recherche Data Gouv\|Recherche Data Gouv]] — and explicitly collaborates with EOSC in international data commons alignment.
