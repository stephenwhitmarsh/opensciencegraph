# EMBL — European Molecular Biology Laboratory

## Overview
The European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL) is Europe's flagship intergovernmental organisation for life science research and infrastructure, founded in 1974 and headquartered in Heidelberg, Germany. With six sites across Europe, EMBL performs basic research in molecular biology, operates major open-access biological data resources, trains scientists, and develops new technologies.

For open neuroscience and the broader life sciences data ecosystem, EMBL's most consequential role is through **EMBL-EBI** (European Bioinformatics Institute, Hinxton, UK), which operates the world's most comprehensive suite of free biological databases and data services — including several repositories central to this vault.

## EMBL Sites
- **EMBL Heidelberg** (Germany) — headquarters; structural biology, cell biology, genome biology, developmental biology; hosts Bio-Hub of [[04_Governance/Euro-BioImaging\|Euro-BioImaging]]
- **EMBL-EBI** (Hinxton, UK) — European Bioinformatics Institute; operates all major European biological databases
- **EMBL Rome** (Italy) — mouse biology, epigenetics
- **EMBL Grenoble** (France) — structural biology; synchrotron beamlines at ESRF
- **EMBL Hamburg** (Germany) — structural biology; beamlines at PETRA III
- **EMBL Barcelona** (Spain) — tissue biology and disease; single-cell biology

## EMBL-EBI Databases and Services
EMBL-EBI operates the core European data infrastructure for life sciences, including repositories directly referenced in this vault:
- **[[03_Platforms/EGA\|EGA]]** — European Genome-phenome Archive (controlled-access genomics)
- **[[03_Platforms/EVA\|EVA]]** — European Variation Archive (open-access variant data)
- **[[03_Platforms/IDR\|IDR]]** — Image Data Resource (reference microscopy/imaging datasets)
- **ENA** — European Nucleotide Archive (INSDC partner; raw sequences)
- **UniProt** — protein sequence and function database
- **ChEMBL** — bioactive molecules and drug data
- **ArrayExpress** / **BioStudies** — functional genomics data
- **BioImage Archive** — broad biological image data repository
- **Ensembl** — genome browser and annotation (human, mouse, and others)
- **PDBe** — Protein Data Bank in Europe (macromolecular structures)

## Relevance to Neuroscience
EMBL's direct relevance to [[01_Actors/Paris Brain Institute\|Paris Brain Institute]] includes:
- **EMBL Grenoble** — structural biology resources at ESRF (French synchrotron), relevant to structural neuroscience and drug target characterisation
- **EMBL-EBI databases** — EGA (genomics data deposition/access), EVA (variant data), IDR (microscopy data), ENA (sequencing data) are all primary deposition and access points for ICM research outputs
- **EMBL Barcelona** — single-cell and tissue biology directly relevant to BICAN and neurological disease cell type characterisation
