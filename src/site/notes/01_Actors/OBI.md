# OBI — Ontario Brain Institute

## Overview
The Ontario Brain Institute (OBI) is a provincially funded, not-for-profit research organisation established in 2010 to maximise the impact of neuroscience research and establish Ontario as a world leader in brain research, commercialisation, and care. It funds and coordinates five **Integrated Discovery Programs (IDPs)** spanning cerebral palsy, depression, concussion, neurodevelopmental disorders, and youth mental health — pan-Ontario programmes that bring together researchers, clinicians, industry partners, and patients across disciplines.

OBI's central contribution to open neuroscience is **[[03_Platforms/Brain-CODE\|Brain-CODE]]** — a large-scale neuroinformatics platform for the collection, curation, federation, and sharing of multimodal brain research data — and its role as a founding sponsor of the **[[01_Actors/CONP\|CONP]]** (Canadian Open Neuroscience Platform).

## Integrated Discovery Programs
OBI's five IDPs collect standardised multimodal data (clinical, neuroimaging, genetic, molecular, behavioural) using Common Data Elements (CDEs) across brain disorders, enabling cross-disorder comparisons:

| Program | Domain |
|---|---|
| Cerebral Palsy | neurodevelopmental |
| Depression | psychiatric |
| Concussion | neurotrauma |
| Neurodevelopmental Disorders | neurodevelopmental |
| Youth Mental Health | psychiatric |

All IDP data is stored and managed on [[03_Platforms/Brain-CODE\|Brain-CODE]], with standardised consent language enabling de-identified data sharing with external researchers.

## Open Science Leadership

### FAIR Data Pioneer
OBI has implemented [[04_Governance/FAIR Principles\|FAIR Principles]]-aligned data sharing since Brain-CODE's inception in 2012 — one of the earliest institutional FAIR implementations in clinical neuroscience. A 2023 paper in *Frontiers in Neuroinformatics* documents how Brain-CODE operationalises FAIR across all four dimensions.

### CONP Founding Sponsor
OBI / Rotman Research Institute is a founding sponsor of the **[[01_Actors/CONP\|CONP]]** (Canadian Open Neuroscience Platform). All Brain-CODE data releases are registered on the searchable CONP data portal, described with a customised DATS (Data Tags Suite) metadata model.

### Centre for Analytics (CfA)
Established in 2023, the **Centre for Analytics** is powered by Brain-CODE and provides a platform for neuroanalytics technologies, skills development, and industry innovation — translating open data assets into clinical and commercial impact.

### Privacy by Design
Brain-CODE was designed with Privacy-by-Design principles, including advanced Privacy-Preserving Record Linkage (PPRL) for linking Brain-CODE records with external health administrative data (e.g. ICES — Institute for Clinical Evaluative Sciences), enabling population-level linkage while preserving participant privacy.
