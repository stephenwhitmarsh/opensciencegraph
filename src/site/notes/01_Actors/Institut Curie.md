# Institut Curie

## Overview
Institut Curie is one of Europe's leading cancer research and treatment centres, combining a hospital and a research centre in Paris. It is directly relevant to the open neuroscience and bioimaging ecosystem through its **PICT-IBiSA** imaging platform — one of the most prominent advanced microscopy centres in France and a founding node of [[04_Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]]. Institut Curie researchers also participate in [[04_Governance/QUAREP-LiMi\|QUAREP-LiMi]] (via PICT-IBiSA), and its bioinformatics and image data management initiatives connect it to [[01_Actors/OME\|OME]] tools and the broader French open microscopy data infrastructure.

## PICT-IBiSA — Cell and Tissue Imaging Platform
PICT-IBiSA (Plateforme d'Imagerie Cellulaire et Tissulaire — Infrastructure en Biologie, Santé et Agronomie) is one of the largest and most technically advanced imaging platforms in France:
- 40+ microscopy systems in open access across 4 buildings
- Full spectrum: confocal, spinning-disk, super-resolution (STED, PALM/STORM), multiphoton, light-sheet, HCS (Biophenics), electron microscopy, CryoEM
- IBiSA-labelled since 2008; [[04_Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] member since 2011
- Member of [[04_Governance/Euro-BioImaging\|Euro-BioImaging]] ERIC
- Hosts the **Nikon Imaging Centre @ Institut Curie-CNRS** — one of 3 in Europe
- Participates in [[04_Governance/QUAREP-LiMi\|QUAREP-LiMi]] working groups on microscopy QC and metadata

## Image Data Management
- **Curie Image Database (CID) / iManage** — developed with Strand Life Sciences; open to all [[04_Governance/France BioImaging\|France BioImaging]] PICT users; interoperates with [[01_Actors/OME\|OME]] (OMERO)
- **BioImageIT** — open-source framework for integration of image data management with analysis, developed in collaboration with France BioImaging and Inria
- Collaboration with Inria-Serpico (now Inria MathNeuro) on image analysis algorithms for super-resolution microscopy, CLEM, and 4D imaging
