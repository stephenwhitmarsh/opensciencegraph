# Brain/MINDS

## Overview
Brain/MINDS (Brain Mapping by Integrated Neurotechnologies for Disease Studies) is Japan's flagship national brain mapping programme, funded by the Japan Agency for Medical Research and Development (AMED) and launched in 2014. Its primary goal is to produce a comprehensive multi-resolution atlas of the common marmoset (*Callithrix jacchus*) brain — a non-human primate model chosen for its genetic tractability and relevance to human neurological and psychiatric disease. Brain/MINDS generates open multi-modal datasets (MRI, diffusion tractography, two-photon calcium imaging, viral tracing, transcriptomics) and deposits them in a dedicated open-access data portal.

The programme explicitly adopts **open data principles**: all datasets are publicly available via the Brain/MINDS Data Portal and are aligned with international neuroinformatics standards through Japan's [[01_Actors/INCF\|INCF]] node participation.

## Scope and Datasets

### Brain/MINDS (Phase 1, 2014–2023)
- Focus: marmoset brain atlas; molecular, cellular, circuit, and whole-brain levels
- Data types: MRI (structural, diffusion), two-photon microscopy, viral vector tracing, single-cell transcriptomics, behavioural data
- Data portal: https://brainminds.riken.jp — open access, no registration required
- Lead institution: RIKEN Center for Brain Science (RIKEN CBS), Wako

### Brain/MINDS Beyond (Phase 2, 2018–ongoing)
- Extends toward **human brain imaging** at population scale
- Multi-site neuroimaging studies across Japanese research institutes
- Includes psychiatric disorder datasets (schizophrenia, depression, ASD, OCD) as part of the SRPBS Multi-Disorder Neuroimaging Dataset
- SRPBS dataset deposited on **[[03_Platforms/OpenNeuro\|OpenNeuro]]** — one of the largest Japanese contributions to international open neuroimaging infrastructure

## Open Science Infrastructure
- **Brain/MINDS Data Portal** — primary open repository for marmoset atlas data; provides standardised access to multi-modal datasets
- **INCF Japan Node** — Japan's national node in the [[01_Actors/INCF\|INCF]] network; supports adoption of [[02_Standards/BIDS\|BIDS]] and other community neuroinformatics standards
- **BIDS adoption** — Brain/MINDS Beyond datasets follow BIDS-compatible structures; SRPBS dataset on OpenNeuro is BIDS-formatted
- **AMED policy** — AMED open science mandate requires open data deposit for publicly funded research outputs
